## Problem

Two related questions in one line of work: can diffusion models synthesize
*useful* training data (textures diverse and faithful enough to augment
real datasets), and can they do high-resolution generation *efficiently*
(without paying the full pixel-space diffusion cost)?

## Approach

**Texture synthesis.** I trained denoising diffusion probabilistic models
(DDPM) on the Amsterdam Library of Textures (ALOT) to generate artificial
textures that resemble the originals while adding diversity — directly
usable for augmenting texture datasets.

**Latent diffusion.** I implemented latent diffusion models (LDM): a
pretrained autoencoder compresses images into a latent space, diffusion
runs there instead of in pixels, and a cross-attention U-Net conditions
generation on text prompts.

## Architecture & Tech

PyTorch end to end: DDPM noise-schedule training on ALOT; for LDM, a
pretrained autoencoder pair, latent-space diffusion, and cross-attention
conditioning wired into the U-Net. Evaluation on CelebA-HQ and LAION using
FID and Inception Score.

## Results & Impact

- DDPM produced diverse, high-fidelity artificial textures resembling the
  ALOT originals — a practical augmentation source for texture-hungry
  models.
- The LDM implementation reproduced the core efficiency argument: diffusion
  in latent space delivers conditional high-resolution synthesis at a
  fraction of the pixel-space compute, quantified with FID/IS on
  CelebA-HQ and LAION.

## What I'd do next

Measure the downstream effect directly — train a texture classifier with
and without synthetic augmentation — and explore fine-tuning the LDM
text encoder for domain-specific vocabularies.
