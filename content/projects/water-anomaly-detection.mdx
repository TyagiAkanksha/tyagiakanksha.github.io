## Problem

Water treatment plants are cyber-physical systems: dozens of sensors and
actuators driven by industrial controllers, all attackable. A compromised
pump or a spoofed sensor reading can quietly push a plant into an unsafe
state. My master's thesis asks: can we detect those anomalies in real time
from the multivariate sensor streams alone — reliably enough that an
operator would actually trust the alerts?

## Approach

I built on Graph Deviation Networks (GDN, AAAI '21), which learn a graph of
sensor relationships and flag deviations from expected behavior. Reproducing
the paper surfaced the real engineering problems:

- **Training instability.** Stock GDN failed on ~40% of random seeds. I
  traced it to embedding initialization and fixed it with orthogonal
  initialization — eliminating the failures entirely.
- **Single-model brittleness.** Individual models disagree about which
  sensors matter. I built a diverse ensemble (varying seeds, top-k, learning
  rates) that averages per-sensor anomaly scores.
- **One signal isn't enough.** I added a frozen reconstruction decoder
  (GDN-RD) so prediction error and reconstruction error vote together.

## Architecture & Tech

PyTorch training pipeline over three testbeds — SWaT (51 sensors), WADI
(127), and ACWA (7, an extreme low-data benchmark) — with a config-driven
experiment system, five-seed evaluation across Oracle F1 / PA-F1 / LDH F1 /
AUC-ROC, graph-based stage localization for root-cause analysis, an LLM
pipeline that turns raw detections into operator-readable explanations, and
an interactive dashboard for exploring alerts.

## Results & Impact

- Ensemble Oracle F1 **0.854** on SWaT 2015 vs **0.810** for published GDN
  (our GDN reproduction: 0.767) — with PA-F1 0.905 and AUC-ROC 0.892.
- Detected 18/35 attack segments on SWaT; evaluated honestly on WADI and
  ACWA including where low data hurts.
- The stability fix turned a research prototype into something that trains
  reliably — the difference between a paper result and a deployable system.

## What I'd do next

Stream the pipeline against live testbed data, quantify detection latency,
and harden the dashboard into an on-call tool with alert triage and
feedback capture for continual evaluation.
