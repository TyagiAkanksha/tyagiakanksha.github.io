## Problem

Classical steganography hides bits in an image's least-significant noise —
easy to detect, easy to destroy. The harder version: embed an *entire
image* inside another so that (a) the carrier looks untouched to humans
and statistical detectors, and (b) the hidden image survives extraction
intact.

## Approach

An encoder–decoder framework trained end to end: the encoder fuses the
secret image into the cover image; the decoder recovers the secret from
the resulting container. The loss balances two competing objectives —
imperceptibility (container ≈ cover) and recoverability (extracted ≈
secret) — so the network learns where in the cover's structure information
can hide without visible cost.

## Architecture & Tech

PyTorch implementation with joint training of the hiding and reveal
networks. I iterated on the network architecture and training parameters
specifically to push concealment accuracy: deeper fusion layers where
capacity was the bottleneck, and loss re-weighting where artifacts crept
into flat image regions.

## Results & Impact

The optimized framework embeds one image within another with high
imperceptibility and robust recoverability of the hidden data — secure
image-in-image transmission that survives the round trip. The
optimization work measurably increased concealment accuracy over the
initial architecture.

## What I'd do next

Test robustness under real-world channel distortions — JPEG
recompression, resizing, screenshots — and evaluate against learned
steganalysis detectors rather than only statistical ones.
