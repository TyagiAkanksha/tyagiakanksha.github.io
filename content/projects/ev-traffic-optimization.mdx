## Problem

When an ambulance is stuck in traffic, the seconds lost to surrounding
vehicles reacting late — or not at all — cost lives. Lane-level dynamics
are the bottleneck: which vehicle should yield, into which lane, and when.
Fixed traffic rules handle this badly; we wanted to learn it.

## Approach

We modeled lane selection as a reinforcement learning problem with
vehicle-to-vehicle (V2V) communication in the loop: vehicles share state so
the policy can coordinate yielding behavior ahead of the emergency vehicle
rather than reacting to it. Policies were trained with Stable-Baselines3
and evaluated in SUMO across multi-modal traffic environments — from
grid-world abstractions to road networks inspired by real layouts.

## Architecture & Tech

A SUMO simulation stack drives the environments; a Gym-style wrapper
exposes lane-level state (including V2V-shared observations) to SB3
agents. The evaluation suite compares learned policies against baseline
and human-level driving behavior on traversal time and safety metrics.

## Results & Impact

- Learned policies **reduced emergency-vehicle traversal times
  significantly**, surpassing human-level performance on the evaluated
  networks.
- Published as *"Improving Lane Level Dynamics for EV Traversal: A
  Reinforcement Learning Approach"* at **VEHITS 2024** — nominated for the
  **Best Student Paper Award**.

## What I'd do next

Stress the policies under partial V2V adoption (only some vehicles
communicating), add sensor noise, and test sim-to-real transfer on scaled
testbeds.
